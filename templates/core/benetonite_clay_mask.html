Benetonite Clay Mask

Calcium Bentonite Clay is a natural, healing clay derived from volcanic ash deposits, primarily composed of montmorillonite. It is known for its potent detoxifying and cleansing properties due to its high absorption capability. When mixed with water or other liquids, the clay has a negative electrical charge that attracts positively charged toxins, heavy metals, and impurities from the body, making it popular for internal and external uses.

• Appearance: Light gray or cream-colored with a very fine, velvety texture.

• Composition: Primarily calcium, silica, magnesium, potassium, and iron.

• pH Level: Alkaline, around 9-10.

• Consistency: When mixed with liquid, it forms a thick paste that can be applied to the skin.

Comprehensive Guide - Using, Storing, Timing.

Here is a comprehensive guide on using, storing, and timing the application of Benetonite Clay Mask to maximize its benefits

How to Use

For External Use
1 Face Mask:

Mix 1-2 teaspoons of calcium bentonite clay with equal parts of water or apple cider vinegar to form a smooth paste.

Apply the paste evenly to your face or other skin areas, avoiding the eyes and sensitive areas.

Leave it on for 10-20 minutes (10 minutes for sensitive skin, up to 20 minutes for normal skin).

Rinse with warm water and pat dry.

Use 1-2 times per week to maintain clear and smooth skin.

2 Body Wrap:

Mix a larger quantity of clay (1 cup) with water to form a thick paste.

Apply to the body, wrap in a warm cloth, and leave for 20-30 minutes.

Rinse off in the shower. This helps in detoxifying the skin and tightening it.

3 Spot Treatment:

Apply a small amount of clay paste to blemishes or acne.

Leave it overnight and rinse in the morning.

4 Bath Soak:

Add 1/2 to 1 cup of clay to your bathwater and soak for 15-20 minutes.

Helps to detoxify the skin and soothe inflammation.

For Internal Use
1 Digestive Aid:

Mix 1 teaspoon of clay in a glass of water and let it sit for 10-15 minutes.

Drink it on an empty stomach, 1-2 times a week.

Always consult with a healthcare provider before ingesting calcium bentonite clay.

2 Oral Care:

Use a pinch of clay on a wet toothbrush.

Brush gently and rinse well.

Helps to remove toxins from the mouth and freshen breath.

When to Use

• Face Masks: Once or twice a week for normal skin; every 10-14 days for sensitive skin.

• Spot Treatments: As needed to target blemishes.

• Body Wraps: Once a month for detoxification.

• Bath Soaks: Once or twice a month to relax and cleanse the skin.

• Internal Use: No more than once or twice per week, and only with food-grade clay.

How to store

Cool, Dark Place Keep in a Cool, Dry Place: Store the clay in an airtight container, away from direct sunlight and humidity.
Avoid Using Metal Utensils Always use wooden, plastic, or glass bowls and utensils when mixing or storing the clay, as metal can react with the clay and reduce its effectiveness.
Shelf Life Properly stored calcium bentonite clay can last indefinitely since it doesn’t spoil. Just make sure to keep the container sealed when not in use to avoid contamination.
Precautions

Do a Patch Test: If you have sensitive skin, always perform a patch test on a small area before applying it to your face or body.

Avoid Metal Tools: Metal can reduce the effectiveness of the clay, so always use non-metallic tools for mixing.

Consult Before Ingesting: If you’re pregnant, nursing, or have a pre-existing health condition, consult with a healthcare professional before using it internally.

Stay Hydrated: When using clay, especially internally, make sure to drink plenty of water to help flush out toxins.
